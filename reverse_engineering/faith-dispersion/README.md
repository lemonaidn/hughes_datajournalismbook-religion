# Reverse Engineering: Non-Christian Faith Dispersion

This assignment uses data from the 2020 U.S. Religion Census to reproduce findings from Mike McMullen's analysis "The Rise and Geographic Dispersion of Non-Christian Faith Traditions in the United States."

## Data Source

The 2020 U.S. Religion Census
- Collected by the Association of Statisticians of American Religious Bodies (ASARB)
- Data aggregated at the county level
- Over 450 denominations and faith groups included
- Available at: https://www.usreligioncensus.org/

## For Students

Read the report (saved as a PDF in this folder), identify the key quantitative facts and claims (focusing on replicable sentences, not the maps), and write well-commented code to reproduce those findings using the 2020 U.S. Religion Census data.

## Original Report

"The Rise and Geographic Dispersion of Non-Christian Faith Traditions in the United States" by Mike McMullen, published in the 2020 U.S. Religion Census report.

The report analyzes seven non-Christian faith traditions (Bahá'í Faith, Buddhism, Hinduism, Islam, Judaism, Sikhism, and Zoroastrianism) and correlates their geographic concentration with immigrant gateway cities.

## Key Concepts

- **High-concentration areas**: Metropolitan areas with at least 6 houses of worship for each faith group
- **Gateway city typology**: Six types of immigrant gateways developed by Singer et al. (2008)
  - Established gateways: Former, Continuous, Post-WWII
  - 21st-century gateways: Emerging, Re-emerging, Pre-emerging

## Data Preparation

The data for this assignment needs to be extracted from the full 2020 U.S. Religion Census dataset. Students will need to:

1. Download the 2020 U.S. Religion Census data from usreligioncensus.org
2. Filter for the seven non-Christian faith traditions analyzed in the report
3. Identify metropolitan areas with high concentrations (6+ houses of worship)
4. Classify cities according to the gateway typology

Note: This assignment may require additional geographic data to identify metropolitan areas and classify gateway cities according to the Singer et al. typology.

## Reproducible Findings to Consider

The report contains numerous quantitative findings comparing the percentage of high-concentration houses of worship in different gateway city types for each faith tradition. Students should identify specific percentage claims from the tables and reproduce them using the U.S. Religion Census data.
